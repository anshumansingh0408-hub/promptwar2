History of Indian Elections 📜

From independence to the world's largest democracy — a journey through India's electoral milestones.

1947

Independence & Foundation

India gains independence. Universal Adult Franchise established. Every citizen 21+ gets the right to vote (later lowered to 18 in 1989 by the 61st Amendment).

1950

ECI Founded

Election Commission of India founded on January 25, 1950. Sukumar Sen becomes the first Chief Election Commissioner, tasked with organizing the first-ever general election.

1951-52

First General Election

173 million voters. 489 Lok Sabha seats. 1,874 candidates. INC wins 364 seats. Jawaharlal Nehru becomes PM. The largest democratic experiment in human history at that time.

1967

Political Diversity

INC wins with a reduced majority. Regional parties gain significant strength in state elections. This marks the beginning of coalition politics in India.

1971

Indira Gandhi Landslide

INC wins 352 seats on the famous "Garibi Hatao" (Remove Poverty) campaign. Voter turnout reaches 55.3%. A decisive mandate for change.

1975-77

Emergency Period

Emergency declared on June 25, 1975. Elections suspended. Fundamental rights curtailed. Press censored. Democracy restored after 21 months in 1977.

1977

First Non-Congress Government

Janata Party wins 295 seats. Morarji Desai becomes the first non-Congress Prime Minister. Democracy proves its resilience.

1984

Sympathy Wave

INC wins 414 seats — the highest ever by any single party. This came after the assassination of Indira Gandhi. Rajiv Gandhi becomes the youngest PM at age 40.

1993

Voter ID Revolution

Chief Election Commissioner T.N. Seshan introduces Photo Electoral Rolls and the EPIC (Voter ID) card system, revolutionizing voter identification in India.

2004

EVM Adoption

Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs) used in all constituencies for the first time. Paper ballots eliminated. Manmohan Singh becomes Prime Minister.

2013

NOTA Introduced

Supreme Court orders the NOTA (None Of The Above) option on EVMs, giving voters the right to reject all candidates while still participating in the democratic process.

2014

Digital Election

BJP wins 282 seats. Narendra Modi becomes PM. India's first social media election — campaigns go digital. Voter turnout reaches 66.4%, the highest at that time.

2019

Record Turnout

900 million eligible voters — the largest election in human history. BJP wins 303 seats. Voter turnout reaches 67.4%, setting a new record.

2024

18th General Election

96.8 crore registered voters. Conducted in 7 phases. VVPAT (Voter Verifiable Paper Audit Trail) machines deployed alongside EVMs for complete transparency.
