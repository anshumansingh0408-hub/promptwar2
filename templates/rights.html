Voter Rights, Duties & Election Rules ⚖️

Know your rights, understand your duties, and learn the rules that make Indian elections the world's largest democratic exercise.

⚖️

Voter Rights (Article 326)

Constitutional rights of every Indian citizen

  • Right to vote if 18+ Indian citizen
  • Right to free and fair election — guaranteed by the Constitution and enforced by ECI
  • Right to secret ballot — no one can compel you to reveal your vote
  • Right to get name in electoral roll — every eligible citizen can register
  • Right to file complaint with ECI — against malpractice or violations
  • Right to know candidate background — via Form 26 (criminal record, assets, education)
🙋

Voter Duties

Responsibilities of a responsible citizen

  • Register as voter — through NVSP portal or BLO
  • Verify name before election — check your name in the electoral roll
  • Vote on election day — exercise your democratic right
  • Not accept bribes — refuse money, gifts, or inducements from any party
  • Report malpractice to 1950 — or use the cVIGIL app
  • Carry valid ID to booth — EPIC card or approved alternate ID
📜

Aachar Sanhita (Model Code of Conduct)

Rules for parties & candidates during elections

  • Activated when ECI announces election schedule
  • No new government schemes — ruling party cannot announce new policies
  • No government vehicles for campaigns — official machinery cannot be used
  • No religious/caste campaigning — appealing on communal lines is banned
  • Campaign material 200m away from booth — silence zone maintained
  • Candidates must submit expense accounts — all spending is tracked
  • Spending limit: Lok Sabha ₹95 lakh, Vidhan Sabha ₹40 lakh
📢

Election Campaign Rules

Regulations for fair campaigning

  • Campaign ends 48 hours before polling — this is the Silent Period
  • No exit polls until last phase ends — media restriction enforced by ECI
  • No liquor within 48 hours of polling — dry day enforced
  • No paid news — advertisements disguised as news are prohibited
  • All ads pre-certified by Media Certification & Monitoring Committee (MCMC)
🚫

NOTA — None Of The Above

Your right to reject all candidates

  • Introduced 2013 by Supreme Court of India
  • Last option on EVM — appears at the bottom of the candidate list
  • Voters reject all candidates — express dissatisfaction without abstaining
  • NOTA is counted but does not affect result — the candidate with most votes still wins, even if NOTA gets more
