Indian Election Timeline

🗺️

Delimitation & Voter Rolls

Before Election Announcement

The Election Commission of India (ECI) ensures constituencies have equal populations and updates the electoral rolls to include all eligible voters above 18 years of age.

📅

Announcement of Schedule

~45-60 Days Before Polling

The ECI formally announces the election dates, and the Model Code of Conduct comes into immediate effect to ensure fair campaigning.

📝

Filing of Nominations

After Formal Notification

Candidates submit their nomination papers. These papers are heavily scrutinized, and candidates are given a short window to withdraw if they choose.

📢

Election Campaigning

Ends 48 hours before voting

Political parties and candidates present their manifestos and rally for votes. Campaigning strictly stops 48 hours before the polls open.

🗳️

Polling Day(s)

As Per ECI Schedule

Voters cast their ballots at designated stations using Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs) equipped with VVPATs for transparency. Often held in multiple phases.

🔢

Counting of Votes

A Few Days After Polling

EVMs are opened and votes are counted under heavy security and strict supervision of ECI officials across all counting centers simultaneously.

🏆

Declaration of Results

Same Day as Counting

The candidate with the highest number of votes in each constituency is declared the winner via the First-Past-The-Post system.

🏛️

Formation of Government

Post Election

The party or coalition that secures a majority of seats in the Lok Sabha (or Vidhan Sabha for states) is invited by the President/Governor to form the government.
