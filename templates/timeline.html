US Election Timeline

📣

Candidate Announcements

~2 Years Before Election

Candidates typically announce their intention to run for President up to two years in advance. They begin campaigning, building staff, and raising funds early.

🗳️

Primary Elections

Jan - June of Election Year

States hold primaries and caucuses to award delegates to the candidates. These delegates will vote for their candidate at the party convention.

🎉

Party Conventions

Summer of Election Year

Political parties hold national conventions to officially nominate their President and Vice President. The party platform is also formally adopted.

📺

Presidential Debates

September - October

The nominated candidates participate in nationally televised debates. These events allow voters to compare candidates directly on major issues.

🗓️

Election Day

1st Tuesday after 1st Monday in Nov

Citizens across the country cast their ballots for their preferred candidate. These votes actually determine the state's electors for the Electoral College.

🔢

Votes Counted

November

Election officials tabulate all early, mail-in, and Election Day ballots. States then certify the popular vote results, confirming their electors.

🏛️

Electoral College Votes

Mid-December

The electors meet in their respective states to officially cast their votes for President and Vice President based on the state's popular vote.

✅

Congress Certifies Results

January 6

A joint session of Congress convenes to count the electoral votes. The Vice President officially declares the winner of the presidential election.

🎊

Inauguration Day

January 20

The President-elect takes the oath of office and officially begins their term as the President of the United States.
